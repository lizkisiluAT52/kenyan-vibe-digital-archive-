PROJECT OVERVIEW

Kenya is a country rich in cultural diversity, with over 40 ethnic communities, each contributing unique traditions, languages, music, art, and practices. Despite this richness, much of Kenya’s cultural heritage faces the threat of erosion due to globalization, urbanization, and generational disconnect. This project aims to create a digital archive to preserve and celebrate Kenyan culture, making it accessible to both locals and the global audience. The archive will serve as an educational resource, a tool for cultural preservation, and a platform to inspire appreciation and innovation rooted in Kenya’s heritage. The digital archive will leverage modern technology to document and store historical artifacts, oral histories, traditional music, dance, cuisine, architecture, and other cultural expressions. By integrating multimedia formats such as videos, images, and text, the platform will provide an immersive experience for users, offering insights into Kenya’s past, present, and the aspirations of its people.
